About This Demo

A quick county-level risk signal, explained in plain language.

This demo estimates which counties may rank toward the high end of eviction filing risk in the next year, relative to other counties.

How to read this

Think of this tool as a relative ranking. A county at the 90th percentile is estimated to have higher eviction filing risk than about 90% of counties in that same year.

The prediction target is whether a county will land in the highest-risk 25% for next year’s eviction filing rate.

What data patterns are used

The score uses county-level historical patterns:

  • lag_1
  • lag_3_mean_obs
  • lag_5_mean_obs
  • years_since_last_obs

Data source

Princeton Eviction Lab county-level observed/validated dataset (county_proprietary_valid_2000_2018.csv).

Limitations

  • Outcomes end at the 2018 outcome-year.
  • Predictions are county-level only.
  • This is a demo and not a causal model.
  • Scores reflect historical correlations, not guarantees.
  • Do not use this demo for high-stakes decisions.
Technical details

The model is logistic regression. In plain terms, it combines the feature inputs into a weighted sum and converts that value through a sigmoid curve to output a probability-like score between 0 and 1.

Regularization (C=0.1) helps avoid overreacting to noisy patterns, and calibration settings are included as guardrails to keep probability-like outputs more stable.

Training data comes from the Princeton Eviction Lab county-level series, with outcomes available through 2018.
